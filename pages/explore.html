Must Visit Wildlife Destinations in Sri Lanka

YALA NATIONAL PARK

As one of Sri Lanka's largest and most visited national parks, Yala National Park is famous for its variety of animals and birds, including the signature elephants and leopards. This stunning and varied national park has something for everyone, from immersive and exciting safari rides to exploring the park's cultural and historical landmarks, and a number of rivers and beaches.

UDAWALAWE NATIONAL PARK

Situated in the south-central region of Sri Lanka, Udawalawe National Park covers approximately 31,000 hectares. The park is especially well-known for its large herds of elephants, which can frequently be seen roaming through the park. The Walawe Ganga flows through the park and is a crucial water source for the local flora and fauna.



View In Map

SINHARAJA FOREST RESERVE

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a stunning rainforest in Sri Lanka's southwest region, that is renowned for its tall trees and abundant biodiversity. This world heritage site is home to a wide assortment of plant and animal species, including various endemic species that are found in no other place on the planet. It is home to over 50% of Sri Lanka's endemic species of birds, snakes, butterflies, amphibians, and fish.

View In Map

WILPATHTHU NATIONAL PARK

Located on the northwest coast of Sri Lanka, Wilpattu National Park is especially well-known for its numerous leopards, which can frequently be seen roaming the park in search of prey. It is also known for its diverse collection of flora and fauna and, its naturally formed lakes called “Villu,” from which the name of the park has been derived from.


View In Map

KUMANA NATIONAL PARK

The abundance of bird species in Kumana National Park is one of the park's main draws for birdwatchers. A wide range of birds, including both migratory and resident species call the park home. The black-necked stork, lesser adjutant, Eurasian spoonbill, and great thick-knee are some of the rare bird species that can be seen in the park.


View In Map

HORTAN PLAINS

The central hills of Sri Lanka contain the highland plateau known as Horton Plains. Due to its altitude of approximately 2,100 meters above sea level, the climate is typically cool and mild. The region is well-known for its one-of-a-kind and diverse ecosystem. Sambar deer species is one of the most common animals found in these plains. World's End, Chimney Pool, and Baker's Falls are a few of the natural attractions in the region.

MISRISSA WHALE WATCHING

Mirissa is a small town on Sri Lanka's south coast that is well-known for its stunning beaches and diverse marine life. Because the region is home to a variety of species, including sperm whales, blue whales, and spinner dolphins, whale and dolphin watching is one of the most popular things to do in Mirissa. By touring the seas of Mirissa one can anticipate seeing a variety of marine life, including colorful fish schools, sea turtles, and possibly even sharks.

View In Map

GALOYA NATIONAL PARK

Galoya National Park is located in the Ampara District in the Eastern Province of Sri Lanka. The Senanayaka Samudraya, a massive artificial reservoir that was created by damming the Galoya River, is one of the Galoya National Park's main attractions. It is home to a variety of waterbirds, including herons, egrets, and kingfishers, the reservoir is a popular spot for boating and birdwatching.


View In Map

HIKKADUWA MARINE PARK

Hikkaduwa Marine Park is situated on the southwestern shoreline of Sri Lanka. It is well-known for its numerous colorful fish, crabs, lobsters, and sea turtles, as well as its rich coral reefs and diverse marine life. Over 60 different kinds of coral inhabit the reefs of Hikkaduwa Marine National Park, including staghorn and brain corals.




View In Map

MADU GANGA SANCTUARY

The Madu Ganga Sanctuary is a wetland in the Balapitiya region of southern Sri Lanka. It is well-known for its mangrove forests, which are home to a wide range of plants and animals, including over 300 plant species and 240 vertebrate animals. A boat tour of the mangroves in the Madu Ganga Sanctuary gives visitors a chance to see the diverse wildlife that lives there.



View In Map

BELLANWILA BIRD PARK

The Bellanwila Bird Sanctuary is a Ramsar Wetland situated in the Western Province of Sri Lanka. It is home to a wide range of bird species, including migratory and resident species, and it covers approximately 370 hectares. The village of Bellanwila, which is closer to the wetland inspired the sanctuary's name. When migratory birds travel between their breeding grounds and their wintering grounds, this wetland serves as an important stopover.

View In Map

PIGEON ISLAND MARINE PARK

Pigeon Island is a small island off the coast of Nilaveli, a beach town in Sri Lanka's Eastern Province. The island is known for its completely clear waters, white sandy seashores, coral reefs, and rich marine life, making it a famous destination for swimming and snorkeling. Several distinct species of birds call the island home, including the stunning Rock Pigeon, from which the island gets its name.
